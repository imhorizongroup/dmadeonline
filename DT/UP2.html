The Biblical Foundation of Intercession

Job 1:5 — Job rose early every morning to offer sacrifices for each of his children. He did not wait for them to pray. He stood in their place.

Acts 12:5 — Peter was in prison. The church gathered and prayed without ceasing. That night, the chains fell off and the door opened on its own. Peter did not free himself. The intercession of others did.

Exodus 20:5–6 — Iniquity visits through generations. But the same passage reveals that those who love Him and keep His commandments receive mercy to a thousand generations. The pattern can be broken by the one who has the authority to stand.

You are not helpless. You have spiritual authority over your household. These prayers activate it.
